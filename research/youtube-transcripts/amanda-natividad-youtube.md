## Transcript Summary

Amanda discussed her transition from journalism into content marketing and explained how understanding audiences is more important than blindly following SEO tactics.

She shared how SparkToro helps marketers identify audience interests, sources of influence, podcasts, publications, and social accounts that shape purchasing decisions.

A major theme throughout the conversation was that creators should focus on building an audience on rented platforms such as Twitter or LinkedIn while simultaneously building owned assets such as newsletters and personal websites.

Amanda also explained her personal content strategy, how she develops content ideas, the importance of content pillars, and why engagement with followers is a critical part of audience building.

---

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Audience Research Drives Better Content

Rather than guessing what audiences want, marketers should identify:

- Publications they read
- Podcasts they follow
- Social accounts they trust
- Topics they frequently discuss

Understanding these influences allows marketers to create more relevant content.

---

### 2. Content Marketing Starts With Solving Problems

Amanda recommends creating content around:

- Frequently asked questions
- Common customer pain points
- Repeated audience concerns

Good content addresses real problems rather than simply targeting keywords.

---

### 3. Build On Rented And Owned Platforms

Amanda's framework:

Rented Platform:
- Twitter
- LinkedIn
- Instagram

Owned Platform:
- Newsletter
- Personal Website

Creators should use social platforms for discovery while building long-term assets they control.

---

### 4. Content Pillars Create Consistency

Instead of writing about everything, creators should focus on:

- 2–3 core content pillars
- Topics they understand deeply
- Areas they genuinely enjoy discussing

This creates audience clarity and stronger positioning.

---

### 5. Make Yourself Worth Searching For

Amanda explained that SEO is not only about rankings.

A better goal is:

"Create enough value that people actively search for your name."

Strong personal branding increases search demand and long-term visibility.

---

### 6. Validate Ideas Through Social Platforms

Amanda frequently:

- Posts individual thoughts
- Observes engagement
- Expands successful ideas into threads
- Converts popular discussions into blog posts and newsletters

This reduces the risk of creating content nobody wants.

---

### 7. Repurpose Instead Of Reposting

Amanda recommends:

Blog Post → Twitter Thread

Rather than simply sharing a link.

The thread should provide standalone value while encouraging readers to explore the longer content later.

---

### 8. Focus On Affinity, Not Just Reach

Audience growth is important.

However, creators should also create content that makes people:

- Relate to them
- Remember them
- Support them

Affinity creates long-term audience loyalty.

## Research Notes

Most Valuable Insight:

Creators should not rely solely on SEO or algorithms. Instead, they should deeply understand their audience, publish consistently around a few core themes, and use social platforms to validate ideas before investing in long-form content.

Application To B2B SaaS:

A B2B SaaS founder can use LinkedIn to test ideas, build authority, identify audience pain points, and direct interested readers toward owned channels such as newsletters and websites.
