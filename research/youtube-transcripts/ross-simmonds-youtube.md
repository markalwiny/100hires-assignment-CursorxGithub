# Ross Simmonds

## Video Information

**Title:** Put Marketing Back Into Content Marketing

**Speaker:** Ross Simmonds

**Company:** Foundation Marketing

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZvZxuoLQr9U

---

# Key Thesis

Most companies focus too much on creating content and not enough on:

1. Research
2. Creation
3. Distribution
4. Optimization

Ross argues that modern content marketing success comes from mastering all four stages rather than simply publishing blog posts.

---

# Core Framework

## 1. Research

### Search Intent

Ross emphasizes understanding why people search.

Four intent categories:

- Informational
- Commercial Investigation
- Navigational
- Transactional

Questions to ask:

- What are prospects Googling?
- What comparisons are they making?
- What problems are they trying to solve?
- What content formats do they prefer?

---

### Reddit Research

Ross considers Reddit one of the best content research tools.

Methods:

#### Site Search

Search:

site:yourdomain.com

or

site:competitor.com

inside Reddit.

Then sort by:

- Top
- All Time

This reveals the most valuable content ever shared from that website.

---

### Subreddit Research

Visit relevant subreddits.

Sort:

- Top
- All Time

Identify:

- Most upvoted discussions
- Most shared topics
- Highest engagement themes

These reveal proven audience interests.

---

### Content Market Fit

Ross introduces:

**Content Market Fit**

Definition:

Creating content that audiences consistently love, share, engage with, and remember.

Indicators:

- High Reddit engagement
- Strong Facebook group interaction
- High discussion volume
- Repeat sharing

---

### Facebook Group Research

Ross recommends studying:

- Niche communities
- Industry groups
- Professional communities

Look for:

- Highly commented posts
- High reaction counts
- Frequently discussed topics

Then build larger content assets around those themes.

# 2. Creation

## Build Around Audience Demand

Create content based on:

- Proven audience interests
- Real search behavior
- Community discussions

Not assumptions.

---

## NerdWallet Example

Ross highlights NerdWallet's strategy:

They dominate:

- Credit cards
- Loans
- Mortgages
- Personal finance

because they created:

- Top-funnel content
- Middle-funnel content
- Bottom-funnel content

for the entire customer journey.

---

## Stripe Example

Important lesson:

Not every company should prioritize blogs.

Stripe succeeds through:

- Documentation
- Developer resources
- API guides

because engineers prefer documentation over traditional blog content.

---

## Channel Market Fit

Ross introduces another concept:

**Channel Market Fit**

Questions:

- Where does your audience consume information?
- What format do they prefer?

Examples:

- Developers → Documentation
- Marketers → Blogs
- Entrepreneurs → YouTube
- Communities → Reddit/Facebook Groups

---

## Shopify Example

Shopify uses:

- Short YouTube videos
- SERP-focused content
- Educational clips

to capture search demand.

---

# 3. Distribution

## Biggest Content Marketing Mistake

Most companies:

1. Write blog
2. Publish blog
3. Share once
4. Move on

Ross argues this is wrong.

---

## Content Should Be Treated As An Asset

Every content piece should be repurposed.

Example:

Blog Post →

- LinkedIn article
- Carousel
- Twitter thread
- Newsletter
- YouTube video
- Reddit post
- Community discussion

---

## Think Like Disney

Ross repeatedly argues:

Don't think like newspapers.

Think like Disney.

Create content once and distribute it repeatedly across channels.

---

## Distribution Channels

Examples:

- LinkedIn
- Reddit
- Facebook Groups
- Medium
- Newsletters
- Email outreach
- Sales teams
- YouTube
- X/Twitter

---

## Create Once, Distribute Forever

One of Ross's core principles.

A single content asset should generate value for months or years through repurposing.

---

# 4. Optimization

## Create Once, Optimize Forever

Publishing is only the beginning.

Content should continuously improve.

---

## Salesforce Example

Ross uses Salesforce's CRM guide.

Over time Salesforce:

- Updated content
- Added videos
- Added beginner guides
- Refreshed information
- Improved UX

Result:

Maintained strong rankings for years.

---

## Hootsuite Example

A single article published years ago generated:

- Millions of visits
- Continued traffic growth

because it was continually updated and optimized.

---

## Optimization Checklist

Ross recommends:

- Update statistics
- Add new research
- Add videos
- Refresh visuals
- Improve E-E-A-T signals
- Add expert insights
- Expand sections
- Improve internal linking

---

# Business-Focused SEO

Ross argues SEO should not be viewed as:

- Just blogging
- Just keyword research
- Just audits

Instead:

SEO should support broader business goals.

---

## Partnership Link Building

One of his favorite tactics.

Example:

If two companies already integrate together:

- Request relevant links
- Build co-marketing relationships
- Leverage existing partnerships

Much easier than cold outreach.

---

## Acquisition Strategy

Ross discusses acquiring websites and directories.

Examples:

- Industry directories
- Niche content sites
- Existing traffic properties

Benefits:

- Existing backlinks
- Existing rankings
- Existing traffic

rather than building everything from scratch.

# Ross Simmonds' Main Philosophy

Content marketing succeeds when organizations embrace:

1. Research
2. Creation
3. Distribution
4. Optimization

Most companies stop at creation.

Winning companies excel at all four.

# Actionable Takeaways

### Immediate

- Research Reddit for content ideas.
- Study top-performing community discussions.
- Audit audience search intent.

### Next 30 Days

- Repurpose existing content into multiple formats.
- Build a distribution process.
- Refresh high-performing old content.

### Long-Term

- Treat content as a reusable asset.
- Build content around audience demand.
- Develop systems for continuous optimization.
- Focus equally on research, creation, distribution, and optimization.

---

# Overall Message

Ross Simmonds' argument is simple:

**Most brands don't have a content creation problem—they have a content distribution and optimization problem.**

The companies that win are not necessarily those producing the most content. They are the ones that:

- Understand their audience deeply.
- Create the right content.
- Distribute it aggressively.
- Optimize it continuously.
